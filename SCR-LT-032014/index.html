(Project) Managing High-Performing Teams

Who Could Benefit from This Talk?

  • Managers of high-performing teams
  • Managers trying to elevate team performance
  • High performers seeking improved relationships

What Makes a High-Performing Team?

  • Number of commits?
  • Number of pull requests?
  • Number of code additions/deletions (++/--)?
  • Number of passing tests?

Lead Differently

  • Redefine your traditional value-add
  • Your "gift"
  • There is a role for you!

But how?

Provide an atmosphere of trust, learning, empowerment

  • "What" vs. "how"
  • Safe space
  • Meet commitments
  • Example: New Javascript framework

Get out of their way

  • Reduce bureaucratic overhead
  • Suggestion: Replace weekly written status reports with daily standups

Provide shelter from distraction

  • "First line of defense"
  • Channel communications through you
  • Caution: Alienation

Know when (not) to insert yourself

  • Resist "nexus" mentality
  • Tactical problem-solving vs. prioritization
  • Suggestion: Prioritize resolution need

Co-locate ("embed")

  • Respect for creative process
  • Observe working norms
  • Reduce “ivory tower” misconceptions
  • Promote "just-in-time" communication

Address under-performers

  • Most high-performing teams will never let a member fail, but can fatigue from members consistently not pulling their weight
  • Replace under-performing team members to minimize “drain” and resentment

Listen

  • The high performing team is communicating, just maybe not in a way you're used to
  • Use active listening: concentrate on the complete message, not just the words you hear
  • Parrot back what you hear to confirm understanding with the group
  • Ask others to parrot back or summarize the discussion for the group

Above all, lead

  • Even the most high-performing, self-organizing teams need leadership, but not necessarily in the traditional sense
  • High-performers often see management as being needed to fill gaps they can already fill on their own
  • You may feel like you’re unsure where you can add value and the team being uncertain as to why you’re needed


So, how do you lead under these circumstances?
